    10 Active MLB Players Whose Fathers Also Played in the MLB

    Major League Baseball has a rich tradition of family connections, with numerous players following in their fathers' footsteps to reach the pinnacle of the sport. Here, we highlight 10 active MLB players who have continued the legacy of their baseball-playing fathers.

    05 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Major League Baseball has a rich tradition of family connections, with numerous players following in their fathers' footsteps to reach the pinnacle of the sport. Here, we highlight 10 active MLB players who have continued the legacy of their baseball-playing fathers.

    1. Vladimir Guerrero Jr.

    Father: Vladimir Guerrero Sr. Vladimir Guerrero Jr., the young and immensely talented slugger for the Toronto Blue Jays, is the son of Hall of Famer Vladimir Guerrero Sr. Known for his powerful swing and exceptional hitting ability, Guerrero Jr. has quickly emerged as one of the most promising young stars in the MLB.

    2. Fernando Tatis Jr.

    Father: Fernando Tatis Fernando Tatis Jr., the electrifying shortstop for the San Diego Padres, inherited his baseball skills from his father, Fernando Tatis, who enjoyed a successful career as an MLB third baseman. Tatis Jr. has made a name for himself with his impressive power, speed, and defensive prowess.

    3. Cavan Biggio

    Father: Craig Biggio Cavan Biggio, the versatile infielder/outfielder for the Toronto Blue Jays, is the son of Hall of Famer Craig Biggio. Following in his father's footsteps, Cavan has showcased his defensive versatility and solid offensive skills, making him an integral part of the Blue Jays' roster.

    4. Cody Bellinger

    Father: Clay Bellinger Cody Bellinger, the dynamic outfielder/first baseman for the Los Angeles Dodgers, is the son of former MLB utility player Clay Bellinger. Like his father, Bellinger has demonstrated exceptional athleticism and a potent bat, contributing to the Dodgers' success on both sides of the field.

    5. Dee Gordon

    Father: Tom Gordon Dee Gordon, known for his speed and defensive prowess, is the son of former MLB pitcher Tom Gordon. Strange-Gordon has carved out a solid career for himself, showcasing his base-stealing ability and defensive skills for multiple teams in the MLB.

    6. Bo Bichette

    Father: Dante Bichette Bo Bichette, the talented infielder for the Toronto Blue Jays, follows in the footsteps of his father, Dante Bichette, who was a four-time MLB All-Star. Bo has impressed with his hitting prowess and defensive capabilities, adding to the Blue Jays' formidable lineup.

    7. Delino DeShields Jr.

    Father: Delino DeShields Delino DeShields Jr., an outfielder for the Miami Marlins, carries on the legacy of his father, Delino DeShields, who had a successful MLB career as a second baseman and outfielder. Known for his speed and defensive skills, DeShields Jr. continues to make his mark in the MLB.

    8. Dereck Rodríguez

    Father: Iván Rodríguez Dereck Rodríguez, a pitcher in the San Francisco Giants organization, is the son of Hall of Famer Iván Rodríguez. While still establishing himself in the MLB, Rodríguez has shown flashes of talent on the mound, hoping to emulate the success of his illustrious father.

    9. Torii Hunter Jr.

    Father: Torii Hunter Torii Hunter Jr., an outfielder in the Texas Rangers organization, is the son of five-time MLB All-Star Torii Hunter. Following in his father's footsteps, Hunter Jr. aims to make his mark in professional baseball, drawing inspiration from his father's accomplished career.

    10. Kacy Clemens

    Father: Roger Clemens Kacy Clemens, a first baseman in the Toronto Blue Jays organization, is the son of legendary pitcher Roger Clemens. While still working his way up the ranks, Clemens showcases his skills on the field, hoping to eventually reach the MLB and continue his family's baseball legacy.

    These active MLB players have not only inherited their fathers' baseball genes but have also worked hard to carve out their own identities in the sport. Whether it's through their hitting, pitching, fielding, or base-running, these players continue to uphold the tradition of family excellence in Major League Baseball.
