    Early Life and Training

    Born in Zama City, Kanagawa Prefecture, Japan, on April 10th, 1993, Naoya Inoue displayed early promise in the sport of boxing. His journey commenced at the tender age of five, under the guidance of his father and the esteemed Japanese boxing coach, Ushiwaka-san.     Professional Boxing Career

    Upon graduating from high school, Inoue made the pivotal choice to embark on a professional boxing career. In October 2012, he inked a deal with Teiken Promotions and marked his debut. Subsequently, his trajectory in the sport soared, propelling him to the status of a three-division world champion. Inoue showcased his prowess by conquering the bantamweight, super flyweight, and light flyweight divisions with commanding performances.

    Achievements and Recognition

    Inoue’s remarkable achievements have garnered widespread recognition, earning him numerous accolades over the course of his career. Ring Magazine bestowed upon him the prestigious title of “Fighter of the Year” in both 2019 and 2020.
