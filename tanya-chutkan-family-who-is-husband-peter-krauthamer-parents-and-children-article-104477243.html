    Federal judge Tanya Chutkan on Monday placed a partial gag order on Donald Trump in the Georgia election subversion case. It will prevent the former president from criticizing and attacking prosecutors and witnesses.

    Chutkan said that Donald Trump is entitled to criticize the Justice Department generally and assert his belief that the case is politically motivated. She, however, said that he can’t mount a “smear campaign” against prosecutors and court personnel.

    “No other criminal defendant would be allowed to do so, and I’m not going to allow it in this case,” Chutkan said.

    Who is Tanya Chutkan | All on her family:

    Tanya Chutkan is a federal judge appointed by former President Barack Obama. The 61-year-old is a Jamaican-born American lawyer. She is serving as a district judge for the District of Columbia.

    Family:

    Chutkan has a younger brother, Norman, and a younger sister, Robynne. They are physicians. The judge was born to an Indo-Jamaican doctor Winston and an Afro-Jamaican dancer Noelle.

    Tanya Chutkan's mother Noelle is the daughter of Frank Hill, one of the founding fathers of the People's National Party.

    Chutkan is married to Peter A. Krauthamer, who is an American jurist. The 66-year-old serves as a United States district judge for the United States District Court for the District of Columbia. Chutkan and Krauthamer have two sons.

    Trump's attorneys might try to challenge Judge Chutkan's gag order. “He is allowed to make statements the prosecution doesn’t like. That’s part of living with the First Amendment,” the former President's lawyer Peter Lauro said.
