    Biography

    Lonnie Johnson was born in Alabama on October 6, 1949. Most famous for inventing the popular Super Soaker water gun toy, he went on to patent seven dozen other products, including the Johnson Thermo-Electrochemical Converter System, which made Popular Mechanics’ 2009 list of the year’s ten most important inventions. Johnson and MGA Entertainment toy company C.E.O. Isaac Larian both contributed to the development of extremely popular toys (Larian’s company produced Bratz dolls). After earning his bachelor’s degree in mechanical engineering and his master’s in nuclear engineering from Tuskegee University, he worked on the United States Air Force’s stealth bomber and on NASA’s Galileo Jupiter mission.
