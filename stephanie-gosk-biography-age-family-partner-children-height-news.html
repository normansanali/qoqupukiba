    Stephanie Gosk Biography

    Stephanie Gosk is an American journalist and correspondent working for NBC News, based in New York City. She contributes to all platforms of NBC News, including Today, MSNBC and NBC Nightly News.

    Gosk wrote on the shooting of the Orlando Nightclub, the Chicago rally, the trial of former House Speaker Dennis Hastert, the Flint Water Crisis, the escapees from the NY prison and an investigation into crumb artificial turf health.

    Before September 2012, Gosk was based in London’s NBC News headquarters, where she covered the death of Pakistan’s Osama Bin Laden and Libya and Egypt’s Arab Spring in 2011. During the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics and security threats during the 2012 London Summer Olympics, she wrote on LGBT problems in Russia, among other stories.

    Until joining NBC News, Gosk worked for ABC News as a London reporter and as a Boston editor. She flew to Iraq while at ABC and worked independently to film, create and announce pieces for “Good Morning America” and “World News Tonight” while embedded with the U.S. military.

    She has also provided coverage of the September 11th attacks in the Boston area and the Boston Catholic priest case, as well as numerous news incidents across the world and the globe, including the death of Pope John Paul and Hurricane Katrina.
