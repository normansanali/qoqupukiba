    Izzy Gleicher On Big Brother 25

    Izzy Gleicher is excited to be part of Big Brother 25. A self-announced “proud queer woman”, she thinks that representation hasn’t really been at the fullest in Big Brother. Hence, she wants to be a “strong powerful representation for my queer community. And that feels really important to me.”

    Izzy is a huge fan of the show but she only began watching the show in January 2023. About her strategy, she added, “As a musician outside the house, I think it’s really important for my game inside the house to crescendo. I want to start low and just observe people and fit in socially. And then towards the end, I can turn on that comp beast when no one will see it coming.”

    Izzy is ready to take advantage of all that she can gather from the trust she forge with all the cast members. She believes that she is going to win “because when I walk into a room, all eyes tend to go on me.”

    Izzy Gleicher Net Worth

    Izzy Gleicher has a net worth of above $250 thousand. She is a flutist, composer, visual artist, and educator. The Miller Theatre, where she was a featured Pop-Up Concerts soloist, described her as “excellent” and a “rising talent and stand-out performer” to The New York Times. “Isabel’s display of virtuosity, her beautiful attitude, and her stunning musicality inspired me,” comments John Zorn.

    Izzy is a member of the Annapolis Chamber Music Festival, the International Contemporary Ensemble, Wild Up, the new music sinfonietta Ensemble Echappe, and the hip-hop group ShoutHouse. She is a founder member of the group Song Sessions Collective, which comprises four improvisers who use acoustic and electronic instruments, as well as an LED light installation, to perform a constantly-evolving piece of music inspired by the compositional structure of whale songs.

    Izzy frequently performs with the conductorless ensemble One Found Sound, based in San Francisco, as well as the Talea Ensemble, Argento New Music Ensemble, and Contemporaneous in New York City. Additionally, she performs at festivals like Prototype Festival, Mostly Mozart, Big Ears, Opera Omaha’s One Festival, TIME: SPANS Festival, SONIC MATTER, and Sacrum Profanum Festival.

    Izzy has appeared in the Works and Process Series at the Guggenheim Museum, the Composer Portrait Series at the Music of the Americas Society, the Park Avenue Armory’s Martin Creed: The Back Door exhibition, the Clark Institute of Art’s Celebration of Helen Frankenthaler, and the annual An Afternoon of Music and Art event of the American Academy of Arts and Letters.

    The most recent Grammy-nominated album by Wild Up, Julius Eastman Vol. 2: Joy Boy, as well as Julius Eastman Vol. 1: Feminine, both had Isabel featured on them. In addition, The Bells Bow Down by the Aequa, Ilaria Kaila, and Aizuri String Quartet, Shred Offline by synth-driven post-rock outfit Infinity Shred, and The Cormorant and Jackrabbit by Indie rock band San Fermin.

    Izzy was one of the 14 musicians who performed with the music ensemble Wild Up in a June 23 Tiny Desk concert. Somehow they all fit. “I’m one of the best flute players in this city,” she said in her Big Brother season 25 introduction. “I got my master’s degree in flute performance from the Yale School of Music. Being a professional flute player is super competitive. Lots of people play the flute out there.”
