    Early Life and Career

    Jean Todt was born in Pierrefort, a small village in France. His passion for cars and motorsports was evident from a young age. He started his career as a rally co-driver and later transitioned into management roles in motorsports teams.

    Transition to Management

    Todt’s managerial skills were first recognized when he was appointed as the Director of Racing for Peugeot. Under his leadership, Peugeot achieved significant success in the World Rally Championship and the Paris-Dakar Rally.

    Joining Ferrari

    In 1993, Todt joined Ferrari as the General Manager of their racing division. His tenure at Ferrari is often regarded as the golden era of the team, with numerous victories in Formula One.

    CEO of Ferrari

    In 2004, Todt was promoted to the position of CEO of Ferrari. During his time as CEO, Ferrari continued to dominate the Formula One scene, further increasing Todt’s reputation and wealth.

    President of FIA

    In 2009, Todt was elected as the President of the International Automobile Federation (FIA). His role at FIA has significantly contributed to his net worth, with a substantial annual salary and benefits.

    Contributions to Motorsports

    As the President of FIA, Todt has made significant contributions to motorsports. He has implemented numerous safety measures and has been instrumental in promoting electric and hybrid technologies in racing.

    Personal Investments

    Apart from his earnings from the automobile industry, Todt has made several personal investments that have contributed to his wealth. He owns properties in several countries and has invested in various businesses.

    Property Investments

    Todt owns several luxurious properties around the world, including a villa in Saint Tropez and an apartment in Paris. These properties significantly add to his net worth.

    Philanthropy

    Todt is also known for his philanthropic activities. He is the founder of the ICM Brain and Spine Institute and contributes significantly to various charitable organizations.

    ICM Brain and Spine Institute

    The ICM Brain and Spine Institute, founded by Todt, is a leading research center for neurological disorders. Todt’s contributions to the institute demonstrate his commitment to giving back to society.

    Estimated Net Worth in 2024

    Considering Todt’s current assets, investments, and income from his role at FIA, his estimated net worth in 2024 is projected to be around $120 million.

    Factors Influencing Net Worth

    Several factors could influence Todt’s net worth in 2024. These include the performance of his investments, changes in property values, and his continued income from FIA.

    Conclusion

    Jean Todt’s journey from a rally co-driver to the President of FIA is a testament to his passion for motorsports and his exceptional managerial skills. His significant contributions to the automobile industry, coupled with his personal investments and philanthropic activities, have led to an estimated net worth of $120 million in 2024. Despite his wealth, Todt remains committed to advancing motorsports and contributing to society, making him a respected figure in the industry.
