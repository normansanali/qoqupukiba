    Mike Todd Easter Video Worship Or A Blasphemous Affair?

    Many viewers were disturbed by the modern and extravagant performances in the Mike Todd Easter video. Pastor Mike Todd of Transformation Church in Tulsa, Oklahoma has recently come under fire on social media for his extravagant Easter 2023 Ransom service.

    31 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Many viewers were disturbed by the modern and extravagant performances in the Mike Todd Easter video.

    Pastor Mike Todd of Transformation Church in Tulsa, Oklahoma has recently come under fire on social media for his extravagant Easter 2023 “Ransom” service.

    Video clips of the service that were posted online revealed that the congregation was nothing as one would expect from a typical church service. 

    The celebration was more reminiscent of a Hollywood awards ceremony than a religious gathering, featuring performances by Kesha and Beyoncé, stage lighting, and dances that some have compared to Sam Smith’s infamous Unholy song.

    Although clips of the service went viral online, the full Mike Todd Easter video has not been uploaded to YouTube.

    The absence of the full Mike Todd Easter video has led to speculation about what the church may be hiding.

    Since assuming leadership of Transformation Church in February 2015, Mike and Natalie Todd have fostered a large and dedicated congregation drawn to their modern perspectives on faith and prayer.

    However, Mike Todd made headlines last year when he ran his saliva on a churchgoer during a service, causing some to question the church’s practices.

    During this year’s Ransom Easter service, the congregation watched exciting performances and intricate dance routines. 

    In one of the performances, the lyrics of Beyoncé’s song Diva were drastically changed to include the line, “The dragon was an angel turned into a hustle.” 

    Additionally, a Ransom performer replicated Beyoncé’s iconic song Formation by singing, “Okay demons now let’s get in formation.”

    In another segment of the Ransom Easter concert, a singer could be seen wearing the internet’s favorite MSCHF Big Red Boots while singing, “I been getting to the money, everybody mad.” 

    This performance has drawn criticism from some who feel that it was inappropriate for a church service.

    One of the segments during the Ransom service by Transformation Church featured a skit where three performers were seen discussing their bodies in a questionable manner, which has led to widespread criticism. 

    This part of the service has been accused of objectifying women and promoting unhealthy body image. 

    Additionally, the service included a dance performance to Kesha’s song “Die Young”, which has also drawn criticism from some viewers. Despite the backlash, there has been no official statement or response from Mike Todd or Transformation Church.

    Netizens respond to Mike Todd’s Easter service

    While some have praised the energy and excitement of the service, others feel that the church’s focus on entertainment detracts from the spiritual message that they are supposed to deliver.

    The backlash on social media has been intense, with many people expressing their disappointment and concern over the direction that the church is taking. 

    Some have accused the church of promoting a shallow and materialistic message rather than one focused on spirituality and faith.

    Many were shocked that a church would organize such a lavish and modern concert, with some calling it “blasphemous.”

    Mike Todd did, however, announce on his official social media account that the service would be streamed live for one session only before deleting the live stream online. 

    YouTuber Nick Jones speculated that the church did not publish the video online due to potential copyright issues or possibly having something to hide from the public.
